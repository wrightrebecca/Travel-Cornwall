The county of treasures

From the wild beauty of Land’s End to the lush creeks around Falmouth, the landscape of Cornwall is wonderfully diverse. Even the coast differs from place to place: the sweep of Fistral Bay on the north coast is very different from the hidden rocky coves further west.

With over 250 miles of coastline to explore, it’s all too easy to miss inland Cornwall. Try not to, as you’ll miss out on places like Bodmin Moor, scattered with ancient settlements or enchanting woodlands like St Nectan’s Glen. Wherever you go, you’ll see hints of Cornwall’s past, from Neolithic stone circles to medieval fishing ports to the scars of old mines.

Unique stays in Cornwall

Hot Tub Shepherds Hut

This luxury hideaway is located off the beaten track just outside Wadebridge on the North Coast of Cornwall. Paired-back yet sumptuous, with heavy linens and indulgent inside-outside living, this handcrafted hideout's tranquil heart features throughout. With space for two and your fluffy friends, it’s the perfect opportunity for an utterly unique escape in Cornwall.

Location

Withiel, England, United Kingdom

Eco Treehouse

This eco-treehouse is an incredible off-grid retreat. Nestled in a secluded setting amongst the tree-tops above a lake, it's the perfect hideaway for a peaceful escape. The treehouse is kept cosy with a log burner. Guests enjoy exclusive use of the summer house which is the perfect place to enjoy a glass of wine of an evening, as well as the rowing boat that can be taken out on the lake.

Location

Eworthy, England, United Kingdom

Converted Farm Stable

Beautifully nestled on a working farm within the North Devon countryside, this converted stables provides guests with a beautiful blend of countryside living, whilst still being within easy reach to the breathtaking North Cornwall coast. It is a homely retreat with an idyllic blend of character and contemporary... It even has a cinema room with a projector screen.

Location

Holsworthy, Devon, United Kingdom
